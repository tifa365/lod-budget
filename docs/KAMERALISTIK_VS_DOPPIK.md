# Kameralistik vs. Doppik - Two Fundamentally Different Accounting Philosophies

## Kameralistik (Cameralistic Accounting)

Traditional German public sector accounting, focusing on **cash flows**:

- **What it tracks**: Only money in/out (Einnahmen/Ausgaben)
- **When recorded**: When cash actually moves (cash principle)
- **Balance**: Income vs. Expenses for the budget year
- **Assets**: Not systematically tracked
- **Simple question**: "Do we have enough money?"

### Example:
```
School renovation contract: €2.5M
- 2024: €500k paid → recorded as €500k expense
- 2025: €2M paid → recorded as €2M expense (different year!)
- Building value increase: NOT recorded
```

## Doppik (Doppelte Buchführung)

Modern double-entry bookkeeping, tracking **complete financial position**:

- **What it tracks**: Assets, liabilities, income, expenses
- **When recorded**: When obligation occurs (accrual principle)
- **Balance**: Full balance sheet + profit/loss statement
- **Assets**: Systematically valued and depreciated
- **Complex question**: "What's our total financial health?"

### Example:
```
School renovation contract: €2.5M
- 2024: €2.5M liability recorded immediately
- Building asset increases by €2.5M
- Annual depreciation tracked (e.g., €100k/year)
- Shows true cost over building lifetime
```

## Berlin's "Erweiterte Kameralistik"

Berlin's clever hybrid approach:

- **Base**: Still Kameralistik (cash-based)
- **Added**: Cost accounting, performance metrics, partial asset tracking
- **Benefit**: 80% of Doppik insights without expensive system change
- **Saved**: ~€100M in conversion costs other states paid

**Why it matters**: Most German states spent millions converting to Doppik. Berlin achieved similar transparency by extending their existing system - a pragmatic compromise that works!
